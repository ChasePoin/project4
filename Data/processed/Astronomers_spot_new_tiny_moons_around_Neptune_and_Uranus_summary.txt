**Astronomers Spot New Tiny Moons Around Neptune and Uranus**  Astronomers have discovered three
previously unknown moons in our solar system: two around Neptune and one around Uranus. The moons,
located far from their respective planets, were identified using powerful telescopes in Hawaii and
Chile. Neptune now has 16 known moons, while Uranus has 28. The discoveries highlight the potential
for more undetected celestial objects within our solar system.
